How it works

Three small Spring Boot services, fully decoupled — they only talk through Kafka events.

1

Order Service

port 8081 · PostgreSQL

Accepts your request, saves the order, and publishes an order.placed event to Kafka. Rejects duplicate requests using an idempotency key.

2

Payment Service

port 8082 · PostgreSQL

Listens for order.placed, processes the payment, and publishes the result as payment.processed. Failed payments are retried, then sent to a dead-letter queue.

3

Notification Service

port 8083

Listens for payment.processed and dispatches a confirmation or failure notice to the customer (logged in this demo).
